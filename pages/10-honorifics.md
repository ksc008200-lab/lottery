# Ch10: 존댓말 & 반말 — Korean Respect

<h3>The Speech Level Spectrum · 말투 스펙트럼</h3>
    <h3 class="kr">한국어는 누구에게 말하느냐에 따라 완전히 달라집니다!</h3>

    <div style="background:linear-gradient(135deg,#fffbeb,#fff8d6); border:1px solid #f0d060; border-radius:14px; padding:24px 28px; margin:24px 0;">
      <div style="font-size:13px; font-weight:700; letter-spacing:1px; text-transform:uppercase; color:#2c2c2c; margin-bottom:12px;">💡 The Big Picture First!</div>
      <p style="margin:0 0 10px; font-size:15px; color:#2c2c2c;">Korean has two major worlds: <strong>존댓말</strong> (respectful speech — used toward people <em>above</em> you) and <strong>반말</strong> (informal speech — used toward people <em>below</em> you, OR very close friends). Inside 존댓말 there are two levels depending on how formal the situation is.</p>
      <p style="margin:0; font-size:12px; font-family:'Noto Sans KR',sans-serif; color:#6b6b6b; font-style:italic;">한국어에는 두 세계가 있어요: <strong>존댓말</strong>(윗사람을 높여 말하는 것)과 <strong>반말</strong>(아랫사람에게 하는 말, 또는 아주 친한 사이). 존댓말 안에서도 상황에 따라 두 가지로 나뉩니다.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="card red">
      <div class="card-title">⚠️ Golden Rule · 황금 규칙</div>
      <p>As a foreigner learning Korean → always start with <strong>해요체 (~요)</strong>. It's polite, warm, and perfect for almost every situation. Switch to 습니다체 only when the setting is very formal (office presentation, military, etc.).</p>
      <p class="kr">한국어를 배우는 외국인이라면 → 항상 <strong>해요체(~요)</strong>로 시작하세요. 거의 모든 상황에서 안전하고 친근합니다!</p>
    </div>

    <h3>The Magic Conversion Rule · 변환 규칙</h3>
    <div class="card green">
      <div class="card-title">🔄 해요체 → 반말: Just REMOVE 요!</div>
      <p>먹어<strong>요</strong> → 먹어 &nbsp;|&nbsp; 가<strong>요</strong> → 가 &nbsp;|&nbsp; 좋아<strong>요</strong> → 좋아 &nbsp;|&nbsp; 해<strong>요</strong> → 해</p>
      <p style="margin-top:8px; color:#666; font-size:13px;">해요체 → 습니다체: 먹어요 → 먹습니다 &nbsp;|&nbsp; 가요 → 갑니다 &nbsp;|&nbsp; 해요 → 합니다</p>
    </div>

    <h3>Korean Has Separate Honorific Words! · 별도의 높임말이 있어요!</h3>

    <div class="card" style="background:#f5eef8; border-left:4px solid #7d3c98; margin:12px 0;">
      <div class="card-title">🌟 Important! Korean has TWO ways to show respect</div>
      <p><strong>① Speech level</strong> — changing the verb ending (습니다 / ~요 / 반말)</p>
      <p><strong>② Separate honorific vocabulary (높임말)</strong> — completely different words used <em>about</em> or <em>to</em> a respected person</p>
      <p style="margin-top:8px;">This is unique to Korean! When talking about what a senior/elder <em>does</em>, you must use special honorific verbs and nouns — not just change the ending.</p>
      <p class="kr" style="margin-top:6px;">한국어는 말투(어미)만 바꾸는 것이 아니라, 아예 단어 자체가 달라지는 <strong>높임말</strong>이 있어요. 윗사람의 행동을 말할 때 반드시 사용합니다!</p>
    </div>

    <h4 style="margin-top:20px;">③ The Honorific Suffix ~(으)시~ · 높임 접미사</h4>
    <div class="card" style="background:#f0f7ff; border-left:4px solid #1A4A8A; margin:12px 0;">
      <p>Add <strong>~(으)시~</strong> to a verb stem to show that the <em>subject</em> of the sentence deserves respect:</p>
      <p style="margin-top:8px;">가다 → 가<strong>시</strong>다 &nbsp;|&nbsp; 오다 → 오<strong>시</strong>다 &nbsp;|&nbsp; 하다 → 하<strong>시</strong>다 &nbsp;|&nbsp; 읽다 → 읽<strong>으시</strong>다</p>
      <p style="margin-top:8px; color:#C0392B;"><strong>Example:</strong> 선생님이 오<strong>셨</strong>어요. (seon-saeng-nim-i o-<strong>syeoss</strong>-eo-yo) — The teacher has come. (showing respect to teacher)</p>
    </div>
