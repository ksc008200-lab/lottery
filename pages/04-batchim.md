# Ch04: Batchim — 받침

<h3>What is Batchim?</h3>
    <h3 class="kr">받침이란 무엇인가?</h3>
    <p>Many Korean syllables have a <strong>third element</strong> — a final consonant that sits at the <strong>bottom</strong> of the syllable block. This is called <strong>받침 (bat-chim)</strong>, which literally means "support" or "foundation."</p>
    <p class="kr">많은 한국어 음절에는 <strong>세 번째 요소</strong>가 있습니다 — 음절 블록의 <strong>아래</strong>에 위치하는 마지막 자음입니다. 이것을 <strong>받침</strong>이라고 하며, 문자 그대로 "지지대" 또는 "기초"를 의미합니다.</p>

    <div class="combine-row"><div class="combine-piece">가</div><div class="combine-arrow">+</div><div class="combine-piece">ㄹ</div><div class="combine-arrow">=</div><div class="combine-result">갈</div><div class="combine-sound">gal · (to go)</div></div>
    <div class="combine-row"><div class="combine-piece">고</div><div class="combine-arrow">+</div><div class="combine-piece">ㅇ</div><div class="combine-arrow">=</div><div class="combine-result">공</div><div class="combine-sound">gong · (ball!)</div></div>
