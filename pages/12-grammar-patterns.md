# Ch12: Grammar Patterns — 문법 패턴

<div class="tip">
      💡 <strong>Why Grammar Patterns?</strong><br>
      Korean grammar patterns are like <em>power-ups</em>! Once you learn a pattern, you can plug in ANY verb and create hundreds of new sentences instantly. This chapter teaches you the 8 most essential patterns that real Koreans use every single day.
    </div>

    <!-- PATTERN 1 -->
    <h3>Pattern 1 · ~고 싶어요 · I want to ___</h3>
    <div class="card" style="background:#f0f7ff; border-left:4px solid #1A4A8A; margin:16px 0;">
      <div class="card-title">🔧 Formula</div>
      <p style="font-size:18px; text-align:center; padding:10px 0;">
        <span class="hangul-cell" style="font-size:22px;">Verb stem</span> + <span style="color:#C0392B; font-weight:700; font-size:22px;">고 싶어요</span>
      </p>
      <p><em>go sip-eo-yo</em> = want to</p>
    </div>

    <table>
      <thead><tr><th>기본형 (Base)</th><th>발음</th><th>고 싶어요 form</th><th>발음</th><th>English</th></tr></thead>

        <tr><td class="hangul-cell">먹다</td><td>meok-da</td><td class="hangul-cell">먹고 싶어요</td><td>meok-go sip-eo-yo</td><td>I want to eat</td></tr>
        <tr><td class="hangul-cell">가다</td><td>ga-da</td><td class="hangul-cell">가고 싶어요</td><td>ga-go sip-eo-yo</td><td>I want to go</td></tr>
        <tr><td class="hangul-cell">배우다</td><td>bae-u-da</td><td class="hangul-cell">배우고 싶어요</td><td>bae-u-go sip-eo-yo</td><td>I want to learn</td></tr>
        <tr><td class="hangul-cell">자다</td><td>ja-da</td><td class="hangul-cell">자고 싶어요</td><td>ja-go sip-eo-yo</td><td>I want to sleep</td></tr>
        <tr><td class="hangul-cell">마시다</td><td>ma-si-da</td><td class="hangul-cell">마시고 싶어요</td><td>ma-si-go sip-eo-yo</td><td>I want to drink</td></tr>
        <tr><td class="hangul-cell">보다</td><td>bo-da</td><td class="hangul-cell">보고 싶어요</td><td>bo-go sip-eo-yo</td><td>I want to see / I miss you</td></tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

    <div class="card" style="background:#fff8e7; border-left:4px solid #E8C84A; margin:16px 0;">
      <div class="card-title">⭐ Special Note</div>
      <p>보고 싶어요 (bo-go sip-eo-yo) literally means "I want to see" — but when said to a person, it means <strong>"I miss you!"</strong> So romantic! 💕</p>

      <p><strong>A:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">오늘 뭐 먹고 싶어요?</span> (o-neul mwo meok-go sip-eo-yo?) — What do you want to eat today?</p>

      <p><strong>A:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">저도요! 같이 가요!</span> (jeo-do-yo! ga-chi ga-yo!) — Me too! Let's go together!</p>

    <div class="card accent" style="margin:20px 0;">
      <div class="card-title">📝 QUIZ 1 — Can you or can't you? 할 수 있어요? 없어요?</div>
      <p>Fill in with <strong>있어요</strong> or <strong>없어요</strong>:</p>
      <p>1. 나는 한국어 할 수 _____. (I can speak Korean) &nbsp;&nbsp; 2. 나는 날 수 _____. (I cannot fly)</p>
      <p>3. 그는 피아노 칠 수 _____. (He can play piano) &nbsp;&nbsp; 4. 나는 요리 잘 할 수 _____. (I can cook well)</p>
      <p class="kr">정답: 있어요 / 없어요 / 있어요 / 있어요</p>
    </div>

    <h3 style="margin-top:32px;">Pattern 3 · ~아/어야 해요 · I have to / must ___</h3>
    <div class="card" style="background:#f0f7ff; border-left:4px solid #1A4A8A; margin:16px 0;">
      <div class="card-title">🔧 Formula</div>
      <p style="font-size:18px; text-align:center; padding:10px 0;">
        <span class="hangul-cell" style="font-size:22px;">Verb stem</span> + <span style="color:#C0392B; font-weight:700; font-size:22px;">아/어야 해요</span>
      </p>
      <p>Use <strong>아야 해요</strong> when the last vowel is ㅏ or ㅗ | Use <strong>어야 해요</strong> for all others</p>
    </div>

        <tr><td class="hangul-cell">자야 해요</td><td>ja-ya hae-yo</td><td>I have to sleep</td></tr>
        <tr><td class="hangul-cell">먹어야 해요</td><td>meog-eo-ya hae-yo</td><td>I have to eat</td></tr>
        <tr><td class="hangul-cell">일어나야 해요</td><td>il-eo-na-ya hae-yo</td><td>I have to wake up</td></tr>
        <tr><td class="hangul-cell">운동해야 해요</td><td>un-dong-hae-ya hae-yo</td><td>I have to exercise</td></tr>

    <div class="dialog-box" style="background:#f9f9f9; border-radius:12px; padding:20px; margin:12px 0; border:1px solid #e0ddd5;">

      <p><strong>Mom:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">일어나야 해요!</span> (il-eo-na-ya hae-yo!) — You have to wake up!</p>
      <p><strong>Child:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">조금만요...</span> (jo-geum-man-yo...) — Just a little more...</p>
      <p><strong>Mom:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">안 돼요! 학교 가야 해요!</span> (an dwae-yo! hak-gyo ga-ya hae-yo!) — No! You have to go to school!</p>
      <p><strong>Child:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">알았어요... 🥱</span> (al-ass-eo-yo...) — Okay, I know...</p>
    </div>

    <h3 style="margin-top:32px;">Pattern 4 · ~지 않아요 · I don't / not ___</h3>
    <div class="card" style="background:#f0f7ff; border-left:4px solid #1A4A8A; margin:16px 0;">
      <div class="card-title">🔧 Formula — How to say NO in Korean</div>
      <p style="font-size:18px; text-align:center; padding:10px 0;">
        <span class="hangul-cell" style="font-size:22px;">Verb stem</span> + <span style="color:#C0392B; font-weight:700; font-size:22px;">지 않아요</span> &nbsp;&nbsp; OR &nbsp;&nbsp; <span style="color:#C0392B; font-weight:700; font-size:22px;">안</span> + <span class="hangul-cell" style="font-size:22px;">Verb</span>
      </p>
      <p>Both forms mean the same thing! <strong>안 + verb</strong> is more casual and faster to say.</p>

    <h3 style="margin-top:32px;">Pattern 6 · ~는 것 같아요 · It seems like / I think ___</h3>
    <div class="card" style="background:#f0f7ff; border-left:4px solid #1A4A8A; margin:16px 0;">
      <div class="card-title">🔧 Formula — Soft Opinions (Polite Korean way!)</div>
      <p>Koreans rarely state opinions as hard facts. They soften with <span class="hangul-cell">~것 같아요</span> (geot ga-ta-yo).<br>It's like saying "I think..." or "It seems..."</p>
    </div>
