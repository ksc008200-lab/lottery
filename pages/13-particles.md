# Ch13: Particles — 조사

<div class="tip">
      💡 <strong>What is a Particle?</strong><br>
      In English, word <em>order</em> tells you who does what: "The cat ate the fish" vs "The fish ate the cat."<br>
      In Korean, <strong>particles (조사)</strong> are tiny words attached to nouns that tell you their role — subject, object, location, and more. This means Korean word order is surprisingly flexible! 🤯
    </div>

    <!-- TOPIC / SUBJECT PARTICLES -->
    <h3>1 · Topic & Subject Particles · 주제/주어 조사</h3>
    <div class="philosophy-grid" style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:16px; margin:16px 0;">
      <div class="phil-box" style="background:linear-gradient(135deg,#1A4A8A,#0d2b5e);">
        <div class="phil-symbol" style="font-size:32px;">은/는</div>
        <div class="phil-name">Topic Marker</div>
        <div class="phil-name-kr">eun / neun</div>
        <div class="phil-desc">"As for ___" · sets the topic · compare/contrast</div>
      </div>
      <div class="phil-box" style="background:linear-gradient(135deg,#C0392B,#922b21);">
        <div class="phil-symbol" style="font-size:32px;">이/가</div>
        <div class="phil-name">Subject Marker</div>
        <div class="phil-name-kr">i / ga</div>
        <div class="phil-desc">New info · emphasis · "who/what does it"</div>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="card" style="background:#fff8e7; border-left:4px solid #E8C84A; margin:12px 0;">
      <div class="card-title">📌 Rule: When to use 은/는 vs 이/가?</div>
      <table style="margin:8px 0; width:100%;">
        <thead><tr><th>Use 은/는</th><th>Use 이/가</th></tr></thead>
        <tbody>
          <tr><td>Topic already known / general statements</td><td>New information / first mention</td></tr>
          <tr><td>Contrast: "A but B..."</td><td>Specific emphasis on WHO</td></tr>
          <tr><td>나<strong>는</strong> 학생이에요 (I am a student)</td><td>누가? 내<strong>가</strong>! (Who? Me!)</td></tr>
        </tbody>
      </table>
    </div>

      <thead><tr><th>한글</th><th>발음</th><th>English</th><th>Note</th></tr></thead>

        <tr><td class="hangul-cell">저<strong>는</strong> 한국어를 공부해요</td><td>jeo-<strong>neun</strong> han-gu-geo-reul gong-bu-hae-yo</td><td>I study Korean</td><td>topic = me</td></tr>

    <h3 style="margin-top:32px;">2 · Object Particle · 목적어 조사 · 을/를</h3>
    <div class="card" style="background:#f0f7ff; border-left:4px solid #1A4A8A; margin:16px 0;">
      <div class="card-title">🔧 Formula — What is being acted upon?</div>
      <p style="text-align:center; font-size:20px; padding:10px 0;">

      </p>
      <p><strong>을</strong> (eul) → after consonant &nbsp;|&nbsp; <strong>를</strong> (reul) → after vowel</p>

      </tbody>
    </table>

    <div class="card" style="background:#fff8e7; border-left:4px solid #E8C84A; margin:12px 0;">
      <div class="card-title">🧠 Easy Memory Trick!</div>
      <p><strong>에</strong> = just <em>existing</em> there / going there (no action)<br>
      <strong>에서</strong> = <em>doing something</em> there (always with an action verb)</p>
      <p style="margin-top:8px;">🏠 집<strong>에</strong> 있어요 — I'm <em>at</em> home (just being there)<br>
      🍳 집<strong>에서</strong> 요리해요 — I'm <em>cooking</em> at home (action!)</p>
    </div>

    <h3 style="margin-top:32px;">💬 Real Life Dialog · 실생활 대화</h3>
    <div class="dialog-box" style="background:#f9f9f9; border-radius:12px; padding:20px; margin:12px 0; border:1px solid #e0ddd5;">
      <div style="font-weight:700; margin-bottom:12px; color:#1A4A8A;">📱 Text Message Style · 문자 메시지</div>
      <p><strong>A:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">지금 어디에 있어요?</span> (ji-geum eo-di-e iss-eo-yo?) — Where are you right now?</p>
      <p><strong>B:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">저는 카페에 있어요. 공부하고 있어요!</span> (jeo-neun ka-pe-e iss-eo-yo. gong-bu-ha-go iss-eo-yo!) — I'm at a café. I'm studying!</p>
      <p><strong>A:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">어느 카페에서 공부해요?</span> (eo-neu ka-pe-e-seo gong-bu-hae-yo?) — Which café are you studying at?</p>
      <p><strong>B:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">학교 앞 카페에서요. 같이 올래요?</span> (hak-gyo ap ka-pe-e-seo-yo. ga-chi ol-lae-yo?) — At the café in front of school. Want to come together?</p>
      <p><strong>A:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">네! 30분 후에 갈게요!</span> (ne! sam-sip-bun hu-e gal-ge-yo!) — Yes! I'll go in 30 minutes!</p>
