# Ch18: Family — 가족 & 관계

<div class="tip">
      💡 <strong>Family is everything in Korean culture!</strong><br>
      Korea has a deeply Confucian family structure — every relationship has a specific term. Unlike English where "uncle" covers many relatives, Korean has <em>different words</em> depending on whether it's your mother's side or father's side, older or younger. It sounds complex, but it shows how much Koreans value family bonds! 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦
    </div>

      <div class="card-title">⭐ 선배 / 후배 — A Uniquely Korean Concept</div>
      <p><strong>선배 (seonbae)</strong> = someone who joined before you — school, work, sports team — always deserves respect.<br>
      <strong>후배 (hubae)</strong> = someone who joined after you — you guide and protect them.<br><br>
      This hierarchy creates strong bonds and lifelong loyalty. Even years later, a 선배-후배 relationship lasts! You often hear this in K-dramas. 📺</p>

    <!-- FAMILY DIALOG -->
    <h3 style="margin-top:32px;">💬 Talking About Family · 가족 이야기 대화</h3>
    <div class="dialog-box" style="background:#f9f9f9; border-radius:12px; padding:22px; margin:14px 0; border:1px solid #e0ddd5;">
      <div style="font-weight:700; margin-bottom:12px; color:#1A4A8A;">👨‍👩‍👧 Introducing Your Family · 가족 소개</div>
      <p><strong>A:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">가족이 몇 명이에요?</span><br>
      <span style="font-size:13px; color:#666;">(ga-jok-i myeot myeong-i-e-yo?) — How many people are in your family?</span></p>
      <p style="margin-top:8px;"><strong>B:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">네 명이에요. 아버지, 어머니, 언니, 그리고 저요.</span><br>
      <span style="font-size:13px; color:#666;">(ne myeong-i-e-yo. a-beo-ji, eo-meo-ni, eon-ni, geu-ri-go jeo-yo.) — Four people. Father, mother, older sister, and me.</span></p>
      <p style="margin-top:8px;"><strong>A:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">남자형제는 없어요?</span><br>
      <span style="font-size:13px; color:#666;">(nam-ja-hyeong-je-neun eobs-eo-yo?) — No brothers?</span></p>
      <p style="margin-top:8px;"><strong>B:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">없어요. 여자만 있어요! 아버지가 좀 힘드세요. 😄</span><br>
      <span style="font-size:13px; color:#666;">(eobs-eo-yo. yeo-ja-man iss-eo-yo! a-beo-ji-ga jom him-deu-se-yo.) — Nope. Only girls! Dad has it a bit tough. 😄</span></p>
      <p style="margin-top:8px;"><strong>A:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">부모님은 어디 사세요?</span><br>
      <span style="font-size:13px; color:#666;">(bu-mo-nim-eun eo-di sa-se-yo?) — Where do your parents live?</span></p>
      <p style="margin-top:8px;"><strong>B:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">부산에 사세요. 저는 서울에 혼자 살아요.</span><br>
      <span style="font-size:13px; color:#666;">(bu-san-e sa-se-yo. jeo-neun seo-ul-e hon-ja sal-a-yo.) — They live in Busan. I live alone in Seoul.</span></p>
      <p style="margin-top:8px;"><strong>A:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">보고 싶겠다! 자주 내려가요?</span><br>
      <span style="font-size:13px; color:#666;">(bo-go sip-gess-da! ja-ju nae-ryeo-ga-yo?) — You must miss them! Do you go down often?</span></p>
      <p style="margin-top:8px;"><strong>B:</strong> <span class="hangul-cell">명절 때마다요. 추석이랑 설날에 꼭 가요!</span><br>
      <span style="font-size:13px; color:#666;">(myeong-jeol ttae-ma-da-yo. chu-seok-i-rang seol-lal-e kkok ga-yo!) — Every holiday. I always go for Chuseok and Seollal!</span></p>
