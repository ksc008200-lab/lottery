    <div style="color:#fff;font-weight:800;font-size:17px;margin-bottom:8px;">🏝️ 제주어란? What is Jeju-eo?</div>
    <p style="color:#bae6fd;margin:0 0 10px;font-size:14px;">제주 방언(濟州方言), also called <strong style="color:#fff;">제주어</strong>, is spoken on Jeju Island and is so distinct from Standard Korean that UNESCO listed it as a <strong style="color:#fbbf24;">Critically Endangered Language</strong> in 2010.</p>
    <p style="color:#bae6fd;margin:0;font-size:14px;">It preserves ancient Korean sounds and vocabulary lost elsewhere — making it fascinating for language learners!</p>

      <div style="color:#7dd3fc;font-weight:800;font-size:15px;margin-bottom:6px;">돌하르방 — Stone Grandfather</div>
      <p style="color:#bae6fd;font-size:13px;margin:0;">The iconic stone statues of Jeju are named after the Jeju word <strong style="color:#fff;">하르방</strong> (grandfather). These volcanic basalt figures once stood at village entrances to protect against evil spirits. Today they are the symbol of Jeju Island — and a perfect example of Jeju dialect living on in everyday culture!</p>

        <div style="color:#d6d3d1;font-size:12px;">여기 → 이디, 저기 → 저디. The ㅕ/ㅓ vowel often shifts toward ㅣ in Jeju.</div>

        <div style="color:#d6d3d1;font-size:12px;">혼저옵서예 ends in -예, a Jeju honorific ending. ~수다 replaces ~습니다 in formal speech.</div>
