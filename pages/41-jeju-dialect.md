🏝️ 제주어란? What is Jeju-eo?
제주 방언(濟州方言), also called **제주어**, is spoken on Jeju Island and is so distinct from Standard Korean that UNESCO listed it as a **Critically Endangered Language** in 2010.

It preserves ancient Korean sounds and vocabulary lost elsewhere — making it fascinating for language learners!

돌하르방 — Stone Grandfather
The iconic stone statues of Jeju are named after the Jeju word **하르방** (grandfather). These volcanic basalt figures once stood at village entrances to protect against evil spirits. Today they are the symbol of Jeju Island — and a perfect example of Jeju dialect living on in everyday culture!
🎵 제주어 발음 특징 Pronunciation Features

아래아 (·)
Ancient Korean vowel preserved in Jeju. Sounds between ㅏ and ㅡ — unique to this dialect.

어 → 이 변화
여기 → 이디, 저기 → 저디. The ㅕ/ㅓ vowel often shifts toward ㅣ in Jeju.

~수다 어미
혼저옵서예 ends in -예, a Jeju honorific ending. ~수다 replaces ~습니다 in formal speech.
