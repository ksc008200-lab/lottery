# Ch21: Adjectives — 형용사

<div class="tip">
      💡 <strong>Korean adjectives are actually verbs!</strong><br>
      In English, adjectives sit <em>before</em> nouns: "a beautiful girl." In Korean, adjectives behave like verbs — they conjugate! You say <span class="hangul-cell">예뻐요</span> (it's pretty) the same way you say <span class="hangul-cell">가요</span> (I go). This makes Korean adjectives incredibly powerful and flexible! 🎨
    </div>

    <!-- HOW ADJECTIVES WORK -->
    <h3>How Korean Adjectives Work · 형용사의 규칙</h3>
    <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; gap:16px; margin:16px 0;">
      <div class="card" style="background:#f0f7ff; border-left:4px solid #1A4A8A;">
        <div class="card-title">📌 As a Predicate (after noun) — most common!</div>
        <p>Subject + <strong>Adjective + 요</strong></p>
        <p style="margin-top:8px;"><span class="hangul-cell">저 사람은 예뻐요.</span><br>
        <span style="font-size:12px; color:#666;">That person is pretty.</span></p>
        <p style="margin-top:4px;"><span class="hangul-cell">한국어는 재미있어요.</span><br>
        <span style="font-size:12px; color:#666;">Korean is interesting.</span></p>
      </div>
      <div class="card" style="background:#fff0f0; border-left:4px solid #C0392B;">
        <div class="card-title">📌 Before a Noun (modifier form)</div>
        <p>Adjective stem + <strong>ㄴ/은</strong> + Noun</p>
        <p style="margin-top:8px;"><span class="hangul-cell">예쁜 사람</span> (yep-peun sa-ram)<br>
        <span style="font-size:12px; color:#666;">a pretty person</span></p>
        <p style="margin-top:4px;"><span class="hangul-cell">재미있는 책</span> (jae-mi-in-neun chaek)<br>
        <span style="font-size:12px; color:#666;">an interesting book</span></p>
      </div>
    </div>
