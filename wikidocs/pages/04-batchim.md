# Ch4: Batchim — 받침

Chapter 4 · 제4장

## Batchim — The Secret Sound at the Bottom

    받침 — 음절 아래 숨겨진 소리

---

## What is Batchim? · 받침이란?

Many Korean syllables have a **third element** — a final consonant that sits at the **bottom** of the syllable block. This is called **받침 (bat-chim)**, which literally means "support" or "foundation."

많은 한국어 음절에는 **세 번째 요소**가 있습니다 — 음절 블록의 **아래**에 위치하는 마지막 자음입니다. 이것을 **받침**이라고 하며, 문자 그대로 "지지대" 또는 "기초"를 의미합니다.

A Korean syllable block is built like this:

    Top: Consonant (초성)
    Middle: Vowel (중성)
    Bottom: Batchim — optional final consonant (종성)

    Example:
    가 = ㄱ + ㅏ         (no batchim)
    갈 = ㄱ + ㅏ + ㄹ    (batchim: ㄹ)
    공 = ㄱ + ㅗ + ㅇ    (batchim: ㅇ)

---

## The 7 Batchim Sound Groups · 7가지 받침 소리

Here is the most important rule: **No matter which consonant is used as batchim, it can only produce ONE of 7 possible sounds.**

가장 중요한 규칙: **어떤 자음이 받침으로 쓰이든, 7가지 소리 중 하나만 납니다.**

| Sound | Consonants That Make It | Example 한글 | Pronunciation | Meaning |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| ㄱ | ㄱ ㄲ ㅋ | 국 | guk | soup |
| ㄴ | ㄴ | 산 | san | mountain |
| ㄷ | ㄷ ㅅ ㅆ ㅈ ㅊ ㅎ ㅌ | 낮 | nat | daytime |
| ㄹ | ㄹ | 달 | dal | moon |
| ㅁ | ㅁ | 봄 | bom | spring |
| ㅂ | ㅂ ㅍ | 잎 | ip | leaf |
| ㅇ | ㅇ | 강 | gang | river |

> Why only 7? Korean syllables are "closed" — the mouth closes at the end. Each group represents a different mouth position when closing: back of throat (ㄱ), tip of tongue on ridge (ㄴ/ㄷ), tongue curled back (ㄹ), lips closed (ㅁ/ㅂ), back of throat open (ㅇ).

---

## Sound Linking · 연음 (Yeon-eum)

When a batchim is **followed by a vowel**, it "slides" into the next syllable. The batchim becomes the initial consonant of the next syllable.

받침 뒤에 모음이 오면, 받침이 다음 음절의 첫 자음으로 "이동"합니다.

| Written 한글 | How It Sounds | Meaning |
| :---: | :---: | :---: |
| 한국어 | han-gu-geo | Korean language |
| 집이 | ji-bi | house (subject) |
| 밥을 | ba-beul | rice (object) |
| 음악 | eu-mak | music |
| 책이 | chae-gi | book (subject) |
| 옷을 | o-seul | clothes (object) |

    Written: 집이  →  Spoken: 지비 (ji-bi)
    The ㅂ of 집 slides to become the first sound of 이

## Important Pronunciation Rules · 중요 발음 규칙

### Rule 1: Nasalization · 비음화

When ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ batchim meets ㄴ or ㅁ, it becomes a nasal sound.

| Written | Becomes | Pronunciation | Meaning |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| 국물 | 궁물 | gung-mul | broth |
| 밥먹어요 | 밤머거요 | bam-meo-geo-yo | eating rice |
| 한국말 | 한궁말 | han-gung-mal | Korean language |
| 닫는 | 단는 | dan-neun | closing |

### Rule 2: Aspiration · 격음화

When ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ/ㅈ meets ㅎ (or vice versa), they merge into an aspirated sound.

| Written | Pronounced As | Pronunciation | Meaning |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| 좋다 | 조타 | jo-ta | good |
| 많다 | 만타 | man-ta | many/much |
| 넣다 | 너타 | neo-ta | to put in |
| 입학 | 이팍 | i-pak | school entrance |

### Rule 3: Tensification · 경음화

When certain batchim precede ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ/ㅅ/ㅈ, those consonants become tense (doubled).

| Written | Spoken Like | Pronunciation | Meaning |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| 학교 | 학꾜 | hak-kkyo | school |
| 식당 | 식땅 | sik-ttang | restaurant |
| 국밥 | 국빱 | guk-ppap | rice soup |

---

## Double Batchim · 겹받침

Some syllables have **two consonants** as the batchim. Only **one** is pronounced — usually the left one, unless followed by a vowel.

일부 음절에는 받침에 **자음 두 개**가 옵니다. 하나만 발음됩니다 — 보통 왼쪽 자음이며, 뒤에 모음이 오면 오른쪽 자음이 이동합니다.

| Double Batchim | Pronunciation Rule | Example | Sounds Like | Meaning |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| ㄳ | ㄱ pronounced | 넋 | neok | soul |
| ㄵ | ㄴ pronounced | 앉다 | an-da | to sit |
| ㄼ | ㄹ pronounced (mostly) | 여덟 | yeo-deol | eight |
| ㄽ | ㄹ pronounced | 외곬 | oe-gol | one-track |
| ㄾ | ㄹ pronounced | 핥다 | hal-da | to lick |
| ㄿ | ㅂ pronounced | 읊다 | eup-da | to recite |
| ㅀ | ㄹ pronounced | 잃다 | il-ta | to lose |
| ㄺ | ㄱ pronounced | 닭 | dak | chicken |
| ㄻ | ㄹ pronounced | 삶 | sam | life/boiling |
| ㄴㅈ | ㄴ pronounced | 앉아 | an-ja | sitting (+ vowel) |

> **Trick:** When followed by a vowel, the **right** consonant slides to the next syllable.
> 앉아 → 안자 (an-ja) — ㅈ slides right

## Practice · 연습

Read each word aloud and identify the batchim sound:

    1. 학교 (hak-kkyo) — school → batchim: ㄱ
    2. 병원 (byeong-won) — hospital → batchim: ㅇ
    3. 사람 (sa-ram) — person → batchim: ㅁ
    4. 숲 (sup) — forest → batchim: ㅂ (ㅍ→ㅂ)
    5. 읽다 (ik-da) — to read → double batchim ㄺ → ㄱ sound

    💡 Quick Summary:
    · Batchim = final consonant in syllable block
    · Only 7 possible sounds regardless of which consonant
    · Linking: batchim + vowel → slides to next syllable
    · Nasalization: ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ + ㄴ/ㅁ → becomes nasal
    · Double batchim: usually left consonant pronounced; right slides when followed by vowel
